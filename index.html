Projects

  • IoT based Elderly Monitoring System

    In my final year project, I embarked on a transformative journey to create an IoT-based Elderly Monitoring System. This comprehensive solution addresses the pressing need for advanced healthcare management. By ingeniously merging cutting-edge hardware and sophisticated software, I orchestrated a symphony of innovation that redefines real-time health monitoring.

    Central to the project is a meticulously crafted mobile app developed using Flutter and Firebase. I seamlessly integrated this app with specialized hardware, equipped with a diverse array of sensors. These sensors diligently collected real-time health data from patients, seamlessly transmitting it to Firebase's real-time database. I harnessed devices like ESP32 and Arduino IDE to orchestrate this seamless data flow. The sensor suite was designed to measure heart rate, blood oxygen levels (SpO2), ECG data, body temperature, and even detect falls.

    The app's dual user roles, catering to doctors and patients, reflect my commitment to holistic healthcare management. For doctors, the app serves as a comprehensive patient information hub, empowering them to make informed decisions based on real-time data insights. On the patient side, a tailored interface enables them to access and manage their health records with ease. The app's notification system played a pivotal role, instantly alerting both doctors and patients to any critical anomalies – a crucial feature for timely interventions.

    My IoT based Elderly Monitoring System is more than just a project; it's a testament to my dedication to creating impactful solutions that bridge healthcare gaps. By seamlessly merging my app development skills with my hardware integration prowess, I've crafted a solution that stands as a beacon of innovation and a testament to my technical prowess.

  • AL-Khalid 1.0

    AL-Khalid 1.0 stands as a testament to my innovative spirit and technical prowess. This project was an ambitious endeavor that resulted in the creation of a fully-functional tank prototype equipped with advanced features. The tank was designed to revolutionize defense mechanisms with its cutting-edge technology.

    At the heart of AL-Khalid 1.0 is a microcontroller, Arduino, which served as the brain of the tank. This microcontroller was programmed using the Arduino IDE, allowing us to integrate complex functionalities seamlessly. The tank was equipped with a mounted gun, capable of aiming and accurately shooting at targets. This precision was achieved through a camera-enhanced defense system that boasted an impressive accuracy rate of 95%.

    The design and engineering of AL-Khalid 1.0 involved a multidisciplinary approach, combining robotics, electronics, and software development. The tank's motors were carefully selected to ensure smooth movement and precise control. As a result, AL-Khalid 1.0 not only demonstrated my coding skills but also showcased my ability to translate innovative ideas into tangible and functional prototypes.

    The success of AL-Khalid 1.0 extended beyond the prototype stage. This project garnered attention and interest from the Heavy Industry of Taxila, where it was planned for further development on a larger scale. AL-Khalid 1.0 exemplifies my commitment to pushing the boundaries of technology and harnessing its potential to create solutions that make a meaningful impact.

  • ICU-based Object Carrying Robot

    The ICU-based Object Carrying Robot is a testament to my ability to innovate and tackle real-world challenges using robotics and technology. This project aimed to address a critical need within the healthcare industry – efficiently and safely transporting objects within an intensive care unit (ICU) environment.

    Equipped with an ultrasonic sensor, the robot was designed to navigate the ICU autonomously, avoiding obstacles in its path. The sensor provided real-time data to ensure the robot's movement was precise and responsive, enhancing safety within the ICU. The robot's capabilities were seamlessly integrated with a live monitoring system accessible through a web interface.

    This project required proficiency in multiple areas, from hardware integration and sensor programming to web development. The ICU-based Object Carrying Robot demonstrated my ability to bridge the gap between software and hardware, resulting in a tangible solution that addressed a critical need in a healthcare setting.

    The project's success is a testament to my commitment to utilizing technology for the betterment of society. By designing a robot that can navigate the challenging environment of an ICU, I showcased my adaptability, technical skillset, and dedication to solving complex problems with innovative solutions.
